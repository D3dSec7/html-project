Aston Martin DB5

Specifications

  • Engine: The DB5 is powered by a 4.0-liter straight-six engine, producing 282 horsepower. A higher-output Vantage version was also available,
    with 314 horsepower.
  • Transmission: The car features a 5-speed ZF manual gearbox, though a 3-speed automatic option was available.
  • Suspension: The DB5 features independent front suspension and a live rear axle with coil springs, providing a smooth yet sporty ride.
  • Weight: The car weighs around 3,311 lbs (1,502 kg), which is light for a luxury grand tourer of its era.

Performance

  • Acceleration: The standard DB5 can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph (0 to 100 km/h) in about 7.1 seconds, which was impressive for its time.
  • Top Speed: It has a top speed of 145 mph (233 km/h), making it one of the fastest cars of its era.

Design

  • Exterior: The DB5's design is one of the most elegant and timeless in automotive history. It was designed by Carrozzeria Touring of Milan
    and features a classic, flowing body with long, sleek lines. The car's distinctive grille, wire-spoke wheels, and chrome accents give it
    a refined, luxurious appearance.
  • Interior: Inside, the DB5 exudes luxury, with hand-stitched leather seats, wool carpets, and a walnut dashboard. The interior was
    spacious and comfortable, with meticulous attention to detail.

Fun Facts

  1. Bond's Signature Car: The DB5 made its first appearance in the James Bond franchise in the 1964 film *Goldfinger*, driven by Sean Connery as
    Agent 007. Its role in *Goldfinger* and subsequent Bond films, including *Thunderball* (1965), *GoldenEye* (1995), *Skyfall* (2012), and *No Time to
    Die* (2021), solidified the car's connection to the fictional spy.
  2. Special Editions: In 2020, Aston Martin released a limited run of DB5 "Goldfinger Continuation" models, complete with working gadgets from
    the films, including the famous rotating license plate and smoke screen emitter.
  3. Hand-Built: Every Aston Martin DB5 was hand-built, which contributed to its high price and exclusivity. Each car was crafted with meticulous
    attention to detail, making it a true masterpiece of engineering.
